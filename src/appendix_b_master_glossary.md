# B. Master Glossary

> Consolidated from all 26 published modules. Terms are deduplicated and organized A-Z.

## A

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **A/B Testing (Organizational)** | A controlled experiment where two groups within the organization experience different approaches (e.g., different processes, different tools, different support models) and outcomes are compared to determine which approach is more effective. |
| **ABM (Account-Based Marketing)** | Marketing strategy targeting specific high-value accounts with personalized campaigns rather than broad market outreach. |
| **Accuracy** | DQ dimension measuring whether data values correctly represent the real-world entities they describe. |
| **Accrual** | An accounting entry recognizing an expense or revenue that has been incurred but not yet paid or received. In payroll: recognizing salary expense in the period worked, even if payment occurs later. |
| **ACH (Automated Clearing House)** | US payment rail for electronic bank transfers. Standard: 1-2 business days. Same-Day ACH available. NACHA file format. |
| **ACV (Annual Contract Value)** | The annualized revenue value of a single customer contract, used to measure deal size and segment customers. |
| **ADKAR Model** | A change management model developed by Prosci comprising five sequential elements: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, and Reinforcement — used to manage individual-level change progression. |
| **Adoption Curve** | The pattern of how a new system, process, or behavior is adopted over time across a population, typically following an S-curve with innovators, early adopters, early majority, late majority, and laggards. |
| **Annualized method** | Tax calculation method where the current period's earnings are projected to an annual amount, the annual tax rate is looked up, and a proportional amount is withheld. Used in India, Germany, and others. |
| **Attribution Model** | Framework for assigning credit across multiple contributing factors when measuring ROI; prevents double-counting of benefits across analytics initiatives. |
| **Apprenticeship Levy** | UK employer-only tax (0.5% of pay bill) to fund apprenticeship training. Applies to employers with annual pay bill over GBP 3 million. |
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | The annualized value of all active recurring revenue contracts, calculated as MRR x 12; the primary top-line metric for subscription and EOR businesses. |
| **Auto-enrollment** | UK requirement since 2012 for employers to automatically enroll eligible workers in a workplace pension scheme. Minimum: 5% employee + 3% employer. |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **B2B Data Vendor** | A company that collects, maintains, and sells business data (company firmographics, contact information, technographics). |
| **BACS (Bankers' Automated Clearing Services)** | UK payment system with a 3-day processing cycle. Standard for UK payroll. Fixed-width file format (Standard 18). |
| **Balanced Scorecard** | A strategic measurement framework that evaluates performance across four perspectives: financial, operational (internal process), stakeholder (customer), and learning/growth — used to provide a multi-dimensional view of transformation success. |
| **BBG (Beitragsbemessungsgrenze)** | German social security contribution ceiling. Earnings above the BBG are not subject to that contribution. Separate ceilings for pension/unemployment and health/care. |
| **Benefits Realization** | The process of measuring and validating that a change initiative actually delivers the benefits projected in its business case, typically tracked at 6, 12, and 24 months post-completion. |
| **Berufsgenossenschaft** | German professional association administering statutory accident insurance (Unfallversicherung) for employers in a specific industry. |
| **Betriebsrat** | German works council with codetermination rights (Mitbestimmung) on working conditions and information/consultation rights on restructuring plans — legally mandated in German companies with 5 or more employees. |
| **Billing leakage** | Revenue lost when the platform fails to charge a client for all billable items (e.g., missing workers on invoices, unbilled employer costs, or incorrect FX markup). |
| **Bottom-Up Market Sizing** | Estimating market size starting from unit-level data (count of potential customers x average spend) and aggregating upward. |
| **Bridges Transition Model** | A change management model focusing on the psychological transition from old to new, comprising three phases: Ending (letting go of the old), Neutral Zone (the uncertain in-between), and New Beginning (embracing the new). |
| **Bronze layer** | First layer of the medallion architecture. Raw data as received from source systems, preserved immutably. Append-only, original format, partitioned by source and ingestion date. |
| **Bulletin de paie** | French payslip. Must contain approximately 30 mandatory line items including detailed social contribution breakdowns. |
| **Burn Multiple** | Net cash burn divided by net new ARR; measures how efficiently the company converts cash into growth (lower is better). |
| **Burn Rate** | The net monthly cash outflow of the company; total cash expenses minus total cash receipts per month. |
| **Business Case** | Structured document presenting the costs, benefits, risks, and timeline for an analytics investment; used to secure budget approval from executives. |
| **Business Glossary** | An authoritative collection of business term definitions that ensures consistent language across the organization. |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Total sales and marketing cost to acquire a new client, divided by number of new clients acquired in the period. |
| **CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)** | The annualized growth rate of an investment or market over a specified period. |
| **Canonical data model** | The single, agreed-upon schema for representing every core business entity across all systems and countries. Maps diverse source formats into unified structures. |
| **Cash application** | The process of matching incoming client payments (wires, ACH) to outstanding invoices. |
| **Cash Conversion Cycle** | The number of days between when cash goes out (to pay workers, taxes, benefits) and when cash comes in (from client payments); structurally inverted in EOR. |
| **CDC (Change Data Capture)** | Data ingestion pattern that captures every insert, update, and delete from a database transaction log. Provides near-real-time data replication. |
| **Center of Excellence (CoE)** | A centralized team or function that provides specialized expertise, standards, best practices, and governance for a specific capability area (e.g., analytics, compliance, change management) to the broader organization. |
| **Change Capacity** | The organization's ability to absorb and execute change at any given time, determined by management bandwidth, operational team bandwidth, technology resources, and organizational resilience — a finite resource that must be managed. |
| **Change Fatigue** | A state of organizational exhaustion caused by the cumulative impact of too many concurrent or sequential change initiatives, manifesting as disengagement, cynicism, resistance regardless of merit, and declining performance. |
| **Change Portfolio Management** | The practice of managing all organizational change initiatives as a portfolio, considering dependencies, aggregate resource requirements, cumulative organizational impact, and strategic alignment — analogous to investment portfolio management. |
| **Change Readiness** | The degree to which an organization, team, or individual is prepared and willing to undergo a specific change, assessed through engagement levels, trust in leadership, recent change history, and understanding of the change rationale. |
| **Charges patronales** | French term for employer-side social contributions. |
| **Charges salariales** | French term for employee-side social contributions. |
| **CHAPS** | UK same-day real-time gross settlement payment system. High cost (GBP 25-35). Typically for high-value transfers. |
| **Church tax (Kirchensteuer)** | Tax levied in Germany on registered members of recognized churches. 8% (Bavaria/Baden-Wurttemberg) or 9% (other states) of Lohnsteuer. |
| **CLT (Consolidacao das Leis do Trabalho)** | Brazilian labor code governing employment relationships. Highly protective of workers. |
| **Co-employment** | Risk that an EOR arrangement is recharacterized by a court as a joint employment relationship, making the client a co-employer with associated liabilities. |
| **Cohort Analysis** | Grouping customers by acquisition period (cohort) and tracking their behavior (retention, expansion, churn) over time to identify trends. |
| **Comite Social et Economique (CSE)** | French employee representative body (replacing the former CE, CHSCT, and DP) that must be informed and consulted on organizational changes, working conditions, and economic decisions affecting employees. |
| **Competitive Displacement** | Winning a customer away from a competitor, as opposed to winning a new-to-category customer. |
| **Completeness** | DQ dimension measuring whether all required data fields are populated. |
| **Confounding Variable** | An external factor that affects the outcome being measured but is not related to the change initiative itself — e.g., rapid volume growth affecting error rates during a platform migration, making it difficult to isolate the migration's impact. |
| **Conformed Dimension** | A dimension table in the analytics layer that provides a single, consistent representation of a master data entity used across all fact tables. |
| **Consistency** | DQ dimension measuring whether the same data element has the same value across all systems where it appears. |
| **COR (Contractor of Record)** | Service model that manages contractor engagements and compliance on behalf of clients, including classification assessment and payment. |
| **Cost Avoidance** | ROI benefit category where analytics prevents future expenditures (e.g., penalty avoidance, error prevention) rather than generating direct revenue. |
| **Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ)** | Total cost of errors, rework, penalties, and missed opportunities caused by inadequate data or analytics capability; used as ROI baseline. |
| **Cost to Serve** | The fully loaded variable cost of delivering EOR service per active worker per month, including payroll processing, compliance, and partner fees. |
| **Country Scorecard** | A standardized assessment of a country's market attractiveness using weighted criteria (size, growth, regulation, competition). |
| **CRDS (Contribution au Remboursement de la Dette Sociale)** | French social contribution to repay social security debt. Non-deductible from taxable income. |
| **CSG (Contribution Sociale Generalisee)** | French social contribution tax levied on most income. Partially deductible from taxable income. |
| **CTC (Cost to Company)** | Indian compensation concept. Total annual compensation including employer contributions (PF, ESI, Gratuity). Split into Basic, HRA, Special Allowance, and other components. |
| **Cumulative method** | Tax calculation method where each pay period considers year-to-date earnings and tax already paid, adjusting the current period's tax. Used in UK (PAYE), Ireland. |
| **Cutoff** | Deadline for submitting payroll inputs for a given pay period. Typically 7-10 business days before pay date for international payroll. |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Data Catalog** | A centralized repository of metadata that documents data elements, their definitions, sources, owners, quality rules, and lineage. |
| **Data Classification** | The process of categorizing data by sensitivity level (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted) to determine appropriate handling, access, and protection requirements. |
| **Data Drift** | The gradual divergence between the master record in the MDM hub and copies of that record in consuming systems due to synchronization failures or delays. |
| **Data Enrichment** | The process of enhancing existing data records with additional information from external sources. |
| **Data Governance Charter** | The founding document that defines the scope, authority, objectives, and membership of the data governance program. |
| **Data Governance Council** | A cross-functional body that sets data policies, resolves data ownership disputes, and oversees the governance program. |
| **Data Lineage** | The documented path of data from its origin through transformations to its final consumption point, enabling traceability and root cause analysis. |
| **Data Profiling** | The statistical analysis of data to understand its structure, content, quality, and relationships — used to detect anomalies and inform DQ rule design. |
| **Data Quality (DQ)** | The degree to which data meets the requirements for its intended use, measured across dimensions of completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness, uniqueness, and validity. |
| **Data Steward** | A business-domain expert responsible for the quality, accuracy, and governance of master data within their assigned domain. |
| **dbt (data build tool)** | An open-source transformation tool that enables analytics engineers to write SQL-based data models with testing and documentation. |
| **DCAM (Data Management Capability Assessment Model)** | A maturity framework developed by EDM Council for assessing an organization's data management capabilities across multiple dimensions. |
| **DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)** | A valuation methodology that projects future free cash flows and discounts them to present value using the weighted average cost of capital. |
| **Dead letter queue (DLQ)** | A queue where events or messages that fail processing are sent for investigation. Used in event spine architecture for handling schema validation failures. |
| **Deal Desk** | The centralized function responsible for reviewing, structuring, approving, and governing all commercial deals before they are signed. |
| **De-escalation** | Techniques for reducing the intensity of resistance or conflict during a change initiative, including active listening, acknowledging concerns, providing evidence, offering compromises, and addressing root causes rather than symptoms. |
| **Deduplication** | The process of identifying and removing duplicate records within or across data sources. |
| **Deterministic Matching** | Entity resolution approach requiring exact agreement on predefined key fields (e.g., tax ID + country code). |
| **Discount Leverage Effect** | The phenomenon in EOR (and other low-margin businesses) where a small percentage price discount causes a disproportionately large percentage margin reduction. |
| **Discount Rate** | Rate used to calculate present value of future benefits in NPV calculations; typically set at company's weighted average cost of capital (WACC). |
| **Discount Waterfall** | A sequential analysis showing how list price is reduced through each discount stage (segment, promotional, volume, negotiated, post-sale) to arrive at realized price. |
| **DORA metrics** | DevOps Research and Assessment metrics: Deployment Frequency, Lead Time for Changes, Change Failure Rate, Mean Time to Restore. Standard engineering velocity measurements. |
| **DSN (Declaration Sociale Nominative)** | French unified statutory filing that replaced multiple separate declarations. Submitted monthly (5th or 15th depending on company size). |
| **DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)** | Average days between invoice and client payment. Target: less than 30 days. Key metric for accounts receivable health. |
| **Dunning** | The systematic process of communicating with clients about overdue payments, escalating through increasingly firm collection actions. |
| **DUNS Number** | A unique nine-digit identifier assigned by Dun & Bradstreet to every business entity, used globally for entity identification. |

## E

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization; the most common measure of operating profitability for growth companies. |
| **Effective date** | The date from which a change (salary increase, new benefit, termination) takes effect. May differ from the date it was entered in the system. |
| **Effective Dating** | Storing each version of a data value with valid-from and valid-to dates to support historical lookup and audit. |
| **Enterprise Value** | The total value of a company (equity value + net debt); the output of a DCF valuation model. |
| **Entity Resolution** | The process of determining whether two or more data records refer to the same real-world entity, using deterministic and/or probabilistic matching techniques. |
| **EOR (Employer of Record)** | Legal employer on behalf of a client in another country. The EOR's local entity holds the employment contract and carries legal employment risk. |
| **EPFO (Employees' Provident Fund Organisation)** | Indian social security body administering provident fund and pension schemes. |
| **Error budget** | SRE concept: the allowed amount of unreliability within an SLO. If 99.9% availability is the SLO, the error budget is 0.1% downtime per period. |
| **ESI (Employee State Insurance)** | Indian health insurance scheme. Employer contributes 3.25%, employee contributes 0.75% on gross salary up to INR 21,000/month. |
| **ETL/ELT** | Extract-Transform-Load / Extract-Load-Transform — patterns for moving and transforming data between systems. |
| **Event spine** | The chronological stream of all business events across the platform. Captures every state transition (worker onboarded, payroll run processed, payment settled, etc.). |

## F

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Faster Payments (FPS)** | UK near-instant payment system. Typically settles in under 2 minutes. Available 24/7/365. Max GBP 1,000,000 (bank-dependent). |
| **Feature Store** | A centralized repository of curated, well-defined features (derived data attributes) used as inputs to machine learning models. |
| **FGTS (Fundo de Garantia do Tempo de Servico)** | Brazilian government-managed worker savings fund. Employer deposits 8% of salary monthly. 40% penalty on accumulated balance upon involuntary termination. |
| **FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)** | US payroll tax funding Social Security (6.2% each employer/employee) and Medicare (1.45% each). Split between employer and employee. |
| **Finanzamt** | German local tax office responsible for administering income tax and issuing tax assessments. |
| **Firmographics** | Quantitative characteristics of a company: size, revenue, industry, location, founding year — the B2B equivalent of demographics. |
| **Float** | Time gap between receiving client payment and disbursing to workers. Creates interest income opportunity. On $500M annual payroll with 7-day float: approximately $384K annual interest at 4%. |
| **Formation professionnelle** | French employer-only payroll tax funding vocational training. Rate varies by company size. |
| **FP&A (Financial Planning & Analysis)** | The finance function responsible for budgeting, forecasting, variance analysis, and financial modeling. |
| **FUTA (Federal Unemployment Tax Act)** | US federal employer-only tax funding the unemployment insurance system. |
| **FX markup** | Spread added to mid-market exchange rate as revenue. Typically 0.25%-1.5% on EOR platforms. Often 30-50% of total gross margin. |

## G

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **G2N (Gross-to-Net)** | The calculation from gross salary to net pay after all deductions (taxes, social security, voluntary deductions). The core payroll engine operation. |
| **Garden leave** | Period after termination notice where the worker remains employed and paid but is not required to (or is prohibited from) working. Common in UK and Germany. |
| **Garnishment** | Court order requiring an employer to withhold a portion of a worker's pay and remit it to a creditor. Priority rules vary by country. |
| **GL posting** | The process of recording payroll transactions in the general ledger through journal entries (debits and credits). |
| **Gold layer** | Third layer of the medallion architecture. Aggregated, business-ready data optimized for dashboards, ML models, and analytics use cases. |
| **Golden Record** | The single, authoritative, deduplicated representation of a real-world entity, created by merging and reconciling data from multiple sources. |
| **GPV (Gross Payment Volume)** | Total amount processed through the platform (not revenue). Key scale metric. |
| **Gross Margin** | Net revenue minus cost of delivery, divided by net revenue; measures the profitability of the core service before operating expenses. |
| **Gross-up (Net-to-gross)** | Reverse calculation: given a desired net pay amount, calculate the gross pay needed after all deductions. Used for relocation payments, signing bonuses. |
| **Grundfreibetrag** | German basic tax-free allowance. The amount of income exempt from income tax (equivalent to UK personal allowance). |

## H

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index)** | A measure of market concentration calculated as the sum of squared market shares; less than 1,000 = fragmented, greater than 2,500 = concentrated. |
| **HMRC (Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs)** | UK tax authority responsible for income tax, National Insurance, and VAT. |
| **Hub-and-Spoke Model** | An organizational structure with a central hub (providing expertise, standards, governance, and shared services) connected to distributed spokes (embedded in business units or regions, providing context-specific execution). |
| **Hurdle Rate** | Minimum acceptable ROI threshold for analytics investments; varies by company stage and risk tolerance (typically 15-30% for growth-stage companies). |
| **HRA (House Rent Allowance)** | Indian salary component providing tax exemption for housing expenses. Part of the CTC structure. |

## I

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **IBAN (International Bank Account Number)** | Standardized international bank account identifier used in Europe and many other countries. |
| **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** | A data-driven definition of the type of company most likely to buy, retain, and expand — used to filter and prioritize prospects. |
| **IFSC (Indian Financial System Code)** | Bank branch identifier used for electronic payment routing in India. |
| **IMPS (Immediate Payment Service)** | Indian instant payment system. Available 24/7/365. Max INR 500,000 per transaction. API-based. |
| **Imputed income** | Non-cash benefit provided to a worker that must be included in taxable income (e.g., company car, housing). Added to gross for tax purposes but not actually paid in cash. |
| **Internal Rate of Return (IRR)** | The discount rate at which an analytics investment's NPV equals zero; used to compare competing investment opportunities. |
| **Insolvenzgeldumlage** | German employer-only levy funding the insolvency insurance system, which pays workers' wages if their employer becomes insolvent. |
| **Intent Data** | Signals indicating that a company is actively researching a topic, visiting competitor websites, or showing purchase intent. |
| **IR35** | UK tax legislation determining whether a contractor is genuinely self-employed or effectively an employee. Applies to off-payroll working arrangements. |

## K

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Kirchensteuer** | German church tax collected based on religious affiliation. 8% or 9% of Lohnsteuer depending on federal state. |
| **Kotter 8-Step Model** | A change management framework comprising: create urgency, form a guiding coalition, develop vision and strategy, communicate the vision, empower action, generate quick wins, consolidate gains, and anchor in culture. |
| **KPI tree** | Hierarchical structure of metrics where high-level business KPIs decompose into operational sub-metrics. Used for executive reporting and root cause analysis. |

## L

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Lagging Indicator** | A metric that measures the actual outcome of a change initiative (e.g., error rate reduction, cost per payslip improvement) — proves whether the change worked but takes time to materialize. |
| **Lead Scoring** | A methodology for ranking prospects based on their likelihood to convert, using firmographic, behavioral, and intent attributes. |
| **Leading Indicator** | A metric that measures behaviors or activities expected to predict future outcomes (e.g., system usage rates, training completion, process compliance) — provides early warning but does not prove success. |
| **Lock** | Point after which payroll data is frozen for a pay period. Breaking the lock is a severity-1 operational event requiring documented approval. |
| **Lock-break** | Exceptional process of reversing a locked payroll run to make corrections. Requires escalated approval and full documentation. |
| **Lohnabrechnung** | German payslip. Must show gross pay, tax withholdings, social security contributions, and net pay. |
| **Lohnsteuer** | German income tax withheld from wages by the employer. Calculated using official Lohnsteuertabellen (tax tables). |
| **Lohnsteuertabellen** | German official tax tables published by the Bundesfinanzministerium, used by payroll engines to determine monthly Lohnsteuer. |
| **LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)** | The total net profit expected from a customer over the entire relationship, typically discounted to present value. |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | Lifetime value divided by customer acquisition cost; the fundamental unit economics efficiency metric (target greater than 3x). |

## M

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Maker-checker** | Control principle requiring different people to prepare (maker) and approve (checker) a transaction. Fundamental payroll control for segregation of duties. |
| **Managed Payroll** | Outsourced payroll processing where the client retains the legal employment relationship. The provider calculates G2N, generates payslips, and produces filings. PEPM: $15-$80. |
| **MAP (Marketing Automation Platform)** | Software for automating marketing tasks: email campaigns, lead nurturing, scoring, and attribution. Examples: HubSpot, Marketo. |
| **Market Intelligence** | The systematic collection, analysis, and application of data about markets, competitors, and customers to inform strategic decisions. |
| **Master Data** | The core business entities (workers, clients, organizations, contracts, reference codes) that are shared across multiple systems and used as the basis for operational and analytical processes. |
| **Master Data Management (MDM)** | The discipline of processes, governance, and technology that ensures a single, consistent, accurate representation of master data entities across all systems. |
| **MDM Hub** | The central system or platform that stores, manages, and distributes golden records to consuming systems. |
| **Measurement Theater** | The practice of reporting positive metrics that create an illusion of change success while the underlying reality is different — e.g., reporting on-time delivery while ignoring increased error rates. |
| **Medallion architecture** | Data platform design pattern with three layers: Bronze (raw), Silver (standardized), Gold (aggregated). Separates concerns and enables progressive data quality enforcement. |
| **Migration Runbook** | A detailed, step-by-step operational guide for executing a system or platform migration, covering data extraction, transformation, loading, validation, cutover sequencing, communication, and rollback procedures. |
| **Misclassification** | Incorrect classification of a worker as an independent contractor when they should be an employee. Consequences: back-taxes, penalties, retroactive benefits, potential criminal liability. |
| **Monte Carlo Simulation** | A quantitative technique that runs thousands of iterations with randomly varied inputs to produce a probability distribution of outcomes. |
| **MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** | A prospect that has engaged with marketing content or campaigns and meets minimum qualification criteria for sales follow-up. |
| **MSA (Master Service Agreement)** | Contract between platform and client defining service scope, responsibilities, pricing, SLAs, and liability allocation. |
| **Multi-touch attribution** | Marketing attribution model that distributes credit for a conversion across multiple touchpoints in the buyer journey, rather than assigning all credit to one touch. |

## N

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **NACHA** | National Automated Clearing House Association. Governs ACH network rules. Also refers to the fixed-width file format used for ACH payment files. |
| **Net Present Value (NPV)** | Total present value of future benefits minus investment costs; primary metric for evaluating multi-year analytics investments. |
| **NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer)** | Indian payment rail processing transfers in half-hourly batches during business hours (8 AM - 7 PM IST). Most common for regular payroll in India. |
| **Net-to-gross (gross-up)** | Reverse calculation: given a desired net pay amount, calculate the gross pay needed after all deductions. Used for relocation payments and signing bonuses. |
| **NIC (National Insurance Contributions)** | UK social security contributions paid by both employers and employees. Fund state pension, NHS, and unemployment benefits. |
| **NINO (National Insurance Number)** | UK worker identifier for tax and National Insurance purposes. Format: AB123456C. |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | Revenue from existing clients year-over-year, including expansion, contraction, and churn. NRR > 100% means expansion outpaces churn. Key SaaS/EOR metric. |

## O

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Off-cycle run** | Payroll run outside the regular payroll calendar. Triggered by missed workers, urgent corrections, or legal deadlines. Typical cost: $50-$200 per run. |
| **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** | Goal-setting framework pairing qualitative objectives with measurable key results. Used for aligning analytics team goals with business outcomes. |
| **Ondernemingsraad** | Dutch works council with advisory rights (adviesrecht) on significant organizational changes — the employer must request advice, and proceeding contrary to the advice can be challenged in the Enterprise Chamber. |

## P

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **P11D** | UK form reporting benefits in kind (non-cash benefits) provided to workers, submitted to HMRC annually. |
| **P45** | UK tax document issued when a worker leaves employment. Shows total pay and tax for the current tax year. |
| **P60** | UK annual certificate summarizing total pay and deductions for the tax year. Now largely replaced by digital equivalent. |
| **PAN (Permanent Account Number)** | Indian tax identifier. 10-character alphanumeric: 5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter (e.g., ABCDE1234F). |
| **Parallel Run** | A testing strategy where both the old and new systems process the same payroll data simultaneously, and outputs are compared field-by-field to verify the new system's accuracy before cutting over. |
| **PAS (Prelevement a la source)** | French income tax withholding at source, introduced in 2019. Employer deducts income tax from wages monthly. |
| **PASS (Plafond de la securite sociale)** | French social security ceiling. Many French social contributions are calculated on salary up to this ceiling (or multiples of it). |
| **Passive Resistance** | A form of resistance where individuals do not actively oppose the change but fail to genuinely adopt it — using the new system only for simple tasks, maintaining shadow spreadsheets, adding unauthorized review steps, or making informal exceptions. |
| **Payback Period** | The number of months required for a customer's gross margin contribution to recover the cost of acquiring that customer. |
| **Payback Period (ROI)** | Time required for cumulative benefits to equal cumulative investment; shorter payback indicates lower risk and faster value realization. |
| **PAYE (Pay As You Earn)** | UK income tax withholding system using cumulative method. Tax calculated considering year-to-date earnings and tax already paid. |
| **Payment rail** | Infrastructure that moves money between bank accounts. Different rails have different speed, cost, and reliability (ACH, SEPA, BACS, NEFT, PIX, SWIFT, WPS). |
| **PE (Permanent Establishment)** | Tax concept where a foreign company's activities in a country create a taxable presence. Risk for clients using EOR to avoid entity setup. |
| **PEPM (Per Employee Per Month)** | Standard pricing unit for EOR and payroll services. EOR: $300-$700; COR: $29-$99; Managed Payroll: $15-$80. |
| **Perpetuity Growth Rate** | The assumed long-term growth rate used in the perpetuity growth method of terminal value calculation; should not exceed long-term GDP growth. |
| **PF (Provident Fund)** | Indian retirement savings scheme. Employer and employee each contribute 12% of basic salary. Administered by EPFO. |
| **Phase Gate** | A decision checkpoint in a transformation program where the program's progress, quality, risk, and readiness are reviewed and a formal go/no-go decision is made before proceeding to the next phase. |
| **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)** | Data that can identify an individual. In payroll: names, tax IDs, bank accounts, salaries, addresses. Requires encryption, access controls, and retention policies. |
| **Pipeline Coverage Ratio** | The ratio of total pipeline value to sales quota; typically targeting 3-4x coverage. |
| **PIX** | Brazilian instant payment system operated by the Central Bank. Available 24/7/365. Free for individuals. Mandatory for banks. |
| **Portage salarial** | French "umbrella company" concept related to but distinct from EOR. A legal framework allowing freelancers to operate through an intermediary company. |
| **Post-mortem** | Structured analysis after an incident to identify root causes and preventive actions. Should be blameless and focused on systemic improvement. |
| **Price Realization Rate** | Realized price divided by list price, expressed as a percentage; measures how much of the intended price the company actually captures. |
| **Probabilistic Matching** | Entity resolution approach using weighted similarity scores across multiple attributes to calculate the likelihood that two records represent the same entity. |
| **Professional Tax** | Indian state-level employment tax varying by state. Deducted from employee wages. Maximum INR 2,500 per year in most states. |
| **Progressive tax** | Tax system where the rate increases as the taxable amount increases. Most countries use progressive income tax brackets. |
| **Publish-Subscribe (Pub-Sub)** | An event-driven integration pattern where a producer publishes events to a topic and consumers subscribe to receive them asynchronously. |

## Q

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Quick Ratio (SaaS)** | (New ARR + Expansion ARR) divided by (Churned ARR + Contracted ARR); measures growth quality by comparing inflows to outflows. |
| **Quick Win** | An early, visible success delivered in the first 30-60 days of a change initiative, designed to build momentum, demonstrate value, and reduce resistance by showing tangible positive results. |

## R

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|

## S
