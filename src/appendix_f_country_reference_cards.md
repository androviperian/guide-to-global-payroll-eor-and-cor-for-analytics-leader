# F. Country Reference Cards

> One-page summary for top 15 EOR countries. Each card covers: key facts, employment law highlights, payroll specifics, compliance complexity rating (1-5, where 5 is most complex), and common pitfalls.

## F.1 United States

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | USD |
| **Pay frequency** | Bi-weekly (most common), semi-monthly, weekly, or monthly depending on state |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | IRS (federal), State tax agencies, local jurisdictions |
| **Key statutory filings** | W-2 (annual), 941 (quarterly), state withholding returns, W-4 (employee) |
| **Tax method** | Annualized; federal brackets + state brackets (some states have no income tax) |
| **Social security** | FICA: Social Security 6.2% EE + 6.2% ER (cap ~$168,600); Medicare 1.45% + 1.45% (no cap); Additional Medicare 0.9% above $200K |
| **Key deductions** | Federal income tax, state income tax, FICA, 401(k) pre-tax, health insurance pre-tax |
| **Employer costs** | FICA (ER), FUTA (6% on first $7,000, offset by SUTA credit), SUTA (varies by state), workers' comp |
| **Notice period** | At-will employment in most states; no mandatory notice period (exceptions: WARN Act for mass layoffs) |
| **Termination rules** | At-will employment (employer can terminate for any non-discriminatory reason); Montana is exception. Final pay timing varies by state (immediate in CA). |
| **Probation** | Not a formal legal concept; common practice is 90-day introductory period with no legal distinction |
| **EOR complexity** | 3/5 -- At-will simplifies termination, but federal/state/local tax complexity is significant. 50 states with different rules. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Multi-state tax compliance; nexus creation risk; varied state final pay rules; ACA health insurance requirements; I-9 verification deadlines |

## F.2 United Kingdom

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | GBP |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (most common), weekly |
| **Tax year** | April 6 - April 5 |
| **Tax authority** | HMRC (Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs) |
| **Key statutory filings** | RTI (Real Time Information -- per pay date), P45 (leaver), P60 (annual), P11D (benefits) |
| **Tax method** | Cumulative PAYE -- each period considers YTD earnings and tax paid |
| **Social security** | NIC: Employee Class 1: 8% (1,048-4,189/mo) + 2% above; Employer: 13.8% above 758/mo |
| **Key deductions** | Income tax (PAYE), NIC (employee), student loan repayments, pension (auto-enrollment 5% EE) |
| **Employer costs** | Employer NIC (13.8%), pension (min 3%), Apprenticeship Levy (0.5% if pay bill > 3M) |
| **Notice period** | Statutory minimum: 1 week per year of service (up to 12 weeks). Contractual may be longer. |
| **Termination rules** | Unfair dismissal protection after 2 years of service. Redundancy requires consultation. Protected characteristics apply from day 1. |
| **Probation** | Typically 3-6 months contractual; reduced unfair dismissal protection during first 2 years |
| **EOR complexity** | 2/5 -- Well-established EOR model, clear PAYE system, English language. RTI real-time filing adds operational discipline. |
| **Common pitfalls** | RTI filing must be on or before pay date; auto-enrollment compliance; IR35 off-payroll rules for contractors; holiday pay calculation (Working Time Regulations) |

## F.3 Germany

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | EUR |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | Finanzamt (local tax office) |
| **Key statutory filings** | Lohnsteuer (monthly, by 10th), SV reports (monthly), annual Lohnsteuerbescheinigung |
| **Tax method** | Continuous progressive formula (not discrete brackets). Steuerklasse I-VI determines withholding. |
| **Social security** | Five branches: Pension 9.3% EE + 9.3% ER; Health ~8.1% EE + ~8.1% ER; Unemployment 1.3% + 1.3%; Care 1.7% EE + 1.525% ER; Accident (ER only ~1.3%). BBG ceilings apply. |
| **Key deductions** | Lohnsteuer, Solidaritatszuschlag (most exempt since 2021), Kirchensteuer (8-9% of Lohnsteuer), social security (employee share) |
| **Employer costs** | Social security (ER share), accident insurance, insolvency surcharge, U2 Umlage (maternity levy) |
| **Notice period** | Statutory: 4 weeks to month-end or 15th; increases with tenure to 7 months after 20 years. |
| **Termination rules** | Strong worker protection. Requires valid reason (personal, behavioral, operational). Works council (Betriebsrat) consultation required. Sozialauswahl for redundancy. Labor court challenges common. |
| **Probation** | Up to 6 months; 2-week notice period during probation |
| **EOR complexity** | 4/5 -- Complex social security with ceilings, church tax, works councils, strong labor protection, and rigorous filing requirements. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Steuerklasse misapplication; church tax omission; BBG ceiling recalculation errors; works council requirements for termination; 13th-month salary expectations in some industries |

## F.4 France

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | EUR |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | DGFiP (Direction generale des Finances publiques), URSSAF |
| **Key statutory filings** | DSN (Declaration Sociale Nominative -- monthly, 5th or 15th), annual tax declarations |
| **Tax method** | PAS (Prelevement a la source) since 2019 -- employer withholds income tax monthly based on rate provided by tax authority |
| **Social security** | Extensive: CSG 9.2%, CRDS 0.5%, pension (multiple tranches), health, unemployment, family allocations. Employer charges ~40-45% of gross. |
| **Key deductions** | PAS (income tax), CSG, CRDS, employee social contributions (pension, unemployment) |
| **Employer costs** | Charges patronales (~40-45%): pension, health, family, unemployment, housing, transport (versement mobilite), training (formation professionnelle), apprenticeship tax |
| **Notice period** | 1-3 months depending on role and seniority. Collective agreements may specify longer. |
| **Termination rules** | Very strong worker protection. Must have "real and serious cause." Formal process: preliminary interview, notification letter, mandatory waiting periods. Wrongful dismissal awards can be significant. |
| **Probation** | 2-4 months depending on role category; renewable once |
| **EOR complexity** | 5/5 -- Highest complexity. ~30 mandatory payslip lines, extensive social charges, portage salarial distinction, aggressive labor inspectors, 35-hour workweek, and complex collective bargaining agreements. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Bulletin de paie (payslip) compliance; collective agreement (convention collective) applicability; 35-hour workweek overtime calculation; travail dissimule risk; RTT (reduction du temps de travail) management |

## F.5 India

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | INR |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | April 1 - March 31 |
| **Tax authority** | Income Tax Department, EPFO, ESIC, state authorities |
| **Key statutory filings** | TDS return (quarterly), PF challan (monthly by 15th), ESI (monthly by 15th), Professional Tax (monthly/quarterly by state) |
| **Tax method** | Annualized; employee can choose Old Regime (with exemptions/deductions) or New Regime (lower rates, fewer deductions) |
| **Social security** | PF: 12% EE + 12% ER of basic; ESI: 0.75% EE + 3.25% ER (if salary < INR 21,000/mo); Gratuity provision (4.81% of basic for 5+ year eligible) |
| **Key deductions** | TDS (income tax), PF (employee share), ESI (employee share), Professional Tax |
| **Employer costs** | PF (12% of basic), ESI (3.25%), Gratuity provision, LWF (Labour Welfare Fund -- nominal) |
| **Notice period** | Typically 30-90 days per contract; no statutory minimum for most private sector workers |
| **Termination rules** | No at-will; reasonable grounds required for established workers. Specific rules for factories/shops under state Shop and Establishment Acts. Gratuity payable after 5 years. |
| **Probation** | Typically 3-6 months; can be extended |
| **EOR complexity** | 4/5 -- CTC structure, dual tax regime, state-level Professional Tax variation, PF compliance complexity, and EOR legal gray area (no specific EOR legislation). |
| **Common pitfalls** | CTC structure misunderstanding; Old vs New tax regime selection; state-level Professional Tax rates; PF on variable pay confusion; ESI eligibility threshold changes; 13th-month gratuity provision; HRA exemption calculation |

## F.6 Brazil

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | BRL |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (paid by 5th working day of following month); 13th salary mandatory |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | Receita Federal, Caixa Economica (FGTS), INSS |
| **Key statutory filings** | eSocial (unified digital filing -- real-time), FGTS deposits (monthly), DIRF (annual withholding) |
| **Tax method** | Progressive monthly brackets (IRRF) |
| **Social security** | INSS: Employee 7.5-14% (progressive); Employer: 20% + RAT (1-3%) + Sistema S (~5.8%) |
| **Key deductions** | IRRF (income tax), INSS (employee), union contribution, transport voucher (6% discount) |
| **Employer costs** | INSS ER (20%), RAT/SAT, FGTS (8% monthly deposit), Sistema S, education salary, 13th salary |
| **Notice period** | 30 days + 3 days per year of service (up to 90 days total). Can be worked or paid (indemnified). |
| **Termination rules** | CLT-governed. Involuntary without cause: 40% FGTS penalty + notice + proportional vacation + proportional 13th salary. Courts strongly favor workers. |
| **Probation** | Up to 90 days (45 + 45 extension) |
| **EOR complexity** | 5/5 -- CLT labor code is extremely protective. 13th salary, FGTS system, complex termination costs, eSocial real-time reporting, vacation rules (30 days + 1/3 bonus), and courts that favor workers. |
| **Common pitfalls** | 13th salary (must be paid in 2 installments); vacation accrual + 1/3 bonus; FGTS penalty calculation on termination; eSocial filing errors; collective bargaining agreements; transport and meal voucher requirements |

## F.7 Singapore

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | SGD |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore), CPF Board |
| **Key statutory filings** | IR8A (annual employee tax form), CPF contributions (monthly), SDL (Skills Development Levy) |
| **Tax method** | Annual assessment; employer provides IR8A, employee files own return. No monthly withholding (except for non-residents). |
| **Social security** | CPF: Employee 20% + Employer 17% (rates vary by age and residency; lower for older workers and PRs). Only for citizens and PRs. |
| **Key deductions** | CPF (employee share for citizens/PRs); foreign workers: no CPF |
| **Employer costs** | CPF (17% for citizens/PRs), SDL (0.25% of wages, min $2), FWL (Foreign Worker Levy for work permit holders) |
| **Notice period** | Per contract; statutory minimum 1 day (< 26 weeks) to 4 weeks (> 5 years) if not specified |
| **Termination rules** | Relatively flexible. Retrenchment benefits: minimum 2 weeks per year for 2+ years tenure. No mandatory severance for shorter tenures. |
| **Probation** | Typically 3-6 months per contract |
| **EOR complexity** | 2/5 -- Clear rules, English-language administration, business-friendly environment. CPF complexity for different worker categories is the main challenge. |
| **Common pitfalls** | CPF rate tables by age bracket and residency status; foreign worker levy tiers; no tax withholding for residents (tax clearance for departing foreigners); bonus AWS (Annual Wage Supplement) expectations |

## F.8 Australia

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | AUD |
| **Pay frequency** | Fortnightly (most common), weekly, or monthly |
| **Tax year** | July 1 - June 30 |
| **Tax authority** | ATO (Australian Taxation Office), Fair Work Commission |
| **Key statutory filings** | STP (Single Touch Payroll -- per pay event), Superannuation guarantee (quarterly), PAYG withholding |
| **Tax method** | Progressive brackets with PAYG withholding (weekly/fortnightly/monthly tables) |
| **Social security** | Superannuation: 11.5% employer contribution (increasing to 12% by 2025-26); Medicare Levy: 2% employee |
| **Key deductions** | PAYG withholding (income tax), Medicare Levy, HELP (student loan) repayments, salary sacrifice items |
| **Employer costs** | Superannuation guarantee (11.5%), payroll tax (state-based, 4.75-6.85%), workers' compensation |
| **Notice period** | 1-4 weeks depending on tenure under National Employment Standards (NES). More for over-45s with 2+ years. |
| **Termination rules** | Unfair dismissal protections under Fair Work Act (after minimum employment period). Small Business Fair Dismissal Code. Redundancy pay up to 16 weeks. |
| **Probation** | Minimum employment period: 6 months (small business: 12 months) before unfair dismissal applies |
| **EOR complexity** | 3/5 -- STP real-time reporting, Modern Awards complexity (industry-specific pay/conditions), state-based payroll tax, and superannuation guarantee compliance. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Modern Award interpretation (100+ awards with different rates); STP reporting accuracy; superannuation guarantee charge for late payment; state payroll tax nexus; casual vs. permanent conversion rules |

## F.9 Canada

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | CAD |
| **Pay frequency** | Bi-weekly or semi-monthly (most common) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | CRA (Canada Revenue Agency), provincial tax authorities, Revenu Quebec |
| **Key statutory filings** | T4 (annual), PD7A (monthly remittance), RL-1 (Quebec), ROE (Record of Employment on termination) |
| **Tax method** | Federal + provincial tax (both progressive brackets). Quebec has separate administration. |
| **Social security** | CPP: 5.95% EE + 5.95% ER (2024, on earnings $3,500-$68,500); CPP2 on higher earnings; EI: 1.66% EE + 2.32% ER (1.4x employee rate) |
| **Key deductions** | Federal income tax, provincial income tax, CPP (employee), EI (employee) |
| **Employer costs** | CPP (ER), EI (ER -- 1.4x employee rate), provincial health taxes (varies), workers' compensation |
| **Notice period** | Federal: 2 weeks after 3+ months. Provincial varies (Ontario: 1-8 weeks by tenure). |
| **Termination rules** | Varies by province. Common law reasonable notice (can be substantial). Federal employees under Canada Labour Code. ESA minimums vs. common law entitlements. |
| **Probation** | 3 months typical; provincial ESA minimums apply |
| **EOR complexity** | 3/5 -- Federal/provincial dual system (especially Quebec), varied provincial employment standards, and bilingual requirements in some jurisdictions. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Quebec separate remittance and administration; provincial employment standards variation; statutory holiday differences by province; ROE timing requirements on termination; bilingual documentation for Quebec |

## F.10 Netherlands

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | EUR |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Authority) |
| **Key statutory filings** | Loonaangifte (monthly payroll tax return), annual employee statement (jaaropgave) |
| **Tax method** | Progressive brackets with loonheffing (combined income tax + social insurance premiums) |
| **Social security** | Employee: premiums included in loonheffing. Employer: WW-Awf (~2.64%), WAO/WIA (~7.11%), Zvw (health ~6.68%), sector premiums |
| **Key deductions** | Loonheffing (combined tax + premiums), pension (employee share) |
| **Employer costs** | Social insurance premiums (~18-22%), mandatory pension contributions, holiday allowance provision (8% of annual salary) |
| **Notice period** | 1-4 months depending on tenure. Employer notice is 2x employee notice. |
| **Termination rules** | Two routes: UWV (public agency) for economic/long-term illness, or kantonrechter (court) for personal reasons. Both require valid grounds. Transition payment (transitievergoeding) mandatory. |
| **Probation** | 1 month (< 2 year contracts), 2 months (permanent or > 2 years) |
| **EOR complexity** | 3/5 -- 30% ruling for expats, holiday allowance (8%), complex termination routes, and works council requirements for larger employers. |
| **Common pitfalls** | 8% holiday allowance (vakantiegeld) -- mandatory, typically paid in May; 30% ruling application for qualifying expats; transition payment calculation on termination; works council thresholds |

## F.11 Japan

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | JPY |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard, usually 25th) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | NTA (National Tax Agency), municipal tax offices |
| **Key statutory filings** | Monthly withholding tax returns, year-end adjustment (nenmatsu chosei), social insurance reports |
| **Tax method** | Monthly withholding tables + year-end adjustment. Municipal tax (juumin-zei) calculated on prior year income and collected from June. |
| **Social security** | Employee + Employer each: Health insurance (~5%), pension (~9.15%), employment insurance (EE: 0.6%, ER: 0.95%), workers' comp (ER only), child-rearing contribution (ER only) |
| **Key deductions** | Income tax withholding, resident tax (from June of following year), social insurance (employee share) |
| **Employer costs** | Social insurance (ER share ~15-16%), child-rearing contribution, workers' compensation |
| **Notice period** | 30 days minimum (or pay in lieu). No tenure-based increase. |
| **Termination rules** | Very strong worker protection. "Objectively reasonable" grounds and "socially appropriate" standard. Courts rarely approve dismissal. Typical resolution: negotiated separation. |
| **Probation** | Typically 3-6 months; limited impact on termination rules |
| **EOR complexity** | 4/5 -- Year-end adjustment complexity, dual income tax (national + municipal with time lag), strong labor protections, and cultural expectations around employment permanence. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Year-end adjustment (nenmatsu chosei) errors; municipal tax timing (collected year after earning); bonus social insurance calculation; strong anti-dismissal culture requiring careful termination handling |

## F.12 UAE

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | AED |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard) |
| **Tax year** | No personal income tax |
| **Tax authority** | MOHRE (Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation), WPS |
| **Key statutory filings** | WPS (Wage Protection System -- mandatory electronic salary payment), MOHRE labor card renewals |
| **Tax method** | No personal income tax. No social security for most expats (UAE nationals have GPSSA). |
| **Social security** | UAE nationals only: GPSSA (General Pension and Social Security Authority) -- 5% EE + 12.5% ER. Expats: none. |
| **Key deductions** | Minimal for expats -- no income tax, no social security. DEWS (end-of-service gratuity scheme) for DIFC employees. |
| **Employer costs** | End-of-service gratuity (21 days salary per year for first 5 years, 30 days per year thereafter); medical insurance (mandatory in most emirates) |
| **Notice period** | 30-90 days per contract (30 days statutory minimum under new labor law) |
| **Termination rules** | Relatively employer-friendly. New labor law (2022) allows termination with notice for legitimate reasons. End-of-service gratuity payable. No reinstatement orders. |
| **Probation** | Up to 6 months; 14-day notice during probation |
| **EOR complexity** | 2/5 -- No income tax simplifies payroll. Main complexity: WPS compliance, gratuity calculation, emirate-specific rules, and free zone vs. mainland distinction. |
| **Common pitfalls** | WPS filing deadlines (late salary triggers MOHRE alerts); gratuity calculation disputes; free zone vs. mainland employment differences; visa/work permit sponsorship requirements; health insurance mandates by emirate |

## F.13 South Korea

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | KRW |
| **Pay frequency** | Monthly (standard, usually 25th) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | NTS (National Tax Service), NPS (National Pension Service), NHIS (National Health Insurance) |
| **Key statutory filings** | Monthly withholding tax returns, year-end tax settlement (February), social insurance reports |
| **Tax method** | Monthly simplified withholding + comprehensive year-end settlement |
| **Social security** | National Pension: 4.5% EE + 4.5% ER; Health Insurance: ~3.5% EE + 3.5% ER; Employment Insurance: 0.9% EE + 1.15-1.75% ER; Industrial Accident (ER only) |
| **Key deductions** | Income tax, local income tax (10% of income tax), national pension, health insurance, employment insurance, long-term care insurance |
| **Employer costs** | Social insurance (ER share), industrial accident insurance, severance reserve (8.33% of monthly salary) |
| **Notice period** | 30 days minimum |
| **Termination rules** | Strong worker protection. "Just cause" required for dismissal. Labor Standards Act requires valid grounds. Severance: minimum 30 days pay per year of service (mandatory for 1+ year tenure). |
| **Probation** | Typically 3 months; reduced notice during probation |
| **EOR complexity** | 4/5 -- Dispatched worker regulations limit EOR model (2-year limit on dispatch), mandatory severance system, complex year-end tax settlement, and strong labor protections. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Dispatched worker 2-year limit (conversion to direct employment often required); mandatory severance provision; year-end tax settlement complexity; severance reform implications; holiday pay calculation under Labor Standards Act |

## F.14 Philippines

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | PHP |
| **Pay frequency** | Semi-monthly (15th and end of month -- mandatory at least twice monthly) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | BIR (Bureau of Internal Revenue), SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG |
| **Key statutory filings** | Monthly withholding tax (BIR 1601-C), quarterly returns, annual ITR, SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG monthly |
| **Tax method** | Monthly withholding based on progressive brackets (graduated tax table) |
| **Social security** | SSS: ~4.5% EE + ~9.5% ER; PhilHealth: 2.5% EE + 2.5% ER; Pag-IBIG: 2% EE + 2% ER (capped) |
| **Key deductions** | Withholding tax, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG (employee shares) |
| **Employer costs** | SSS (ER), PhilHealth (ER), Pag-IBIG (ER), 13th-month pay (mandatory), EC (Employees' Compensation) |
| **Notice period** | 30 days for employer-initiated termination |
| **Termination rules** | Two categories: "just cause" (no separation pay) and "authorized cause" (separation pay of 1/2 to 1 month per year). Due process requirements for both. |
| **Probation** | Up to 6 months |
| **EOR complexity** | 3/5 -- Semi-monthly pay requirement, mandatory 13th-month pay, multiple social security systems (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG), and BIR filing complexity. |
| **Common pitfalls** | Semi-monthly pay date compliance; 13th-month pay calculation and timing (must be paid by Dec 24); de minimis benefits tax treatment; BIR alpha list filing; final pay computation on termination (30-day deadline) |

## F.15 Mexico

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Currency** | MXN |
| **Pay frequency** | Bi-monthly (quincena: 15th and last day -- most common) |
| **Tax year** | January 1 - December 31 |
| **Tax authority** | SAT (Servicio de Administracion Tributaria), IMSS (social security) |
| **Key statutory filings** | Monthly ISR withholding, bi-monthly IMSS contributions, annual employer declaration, CFDI payroll receipts (per pay period) |
| **Tax method** | Monthly progressive brackets (ISR); annual adjustment via employer year-end calculation |
| **Social security** | IMSS: Employer ~25-30% of integrated daily salary (SBC); Employee: ~2.5%. Covers: retirement (SAR), housing (Infonavit), disability, medical, childcare. |
| **Key deductions** | ISR (income tax), IMSS (employee share) |
| **Employer costs** | IMSS ER contributions (~25-30% of SBC), Infonavit (5%), state payroll tax (2-3%), Christmas bonus (aguinaldo, 15+ days salary) |
| **Notice period** | No mandatory notice period. Employer must pay indemnification (3 months salary + 20 days per year seniority). |
| **Termination rules** | LFT (Federal Labor Law). "Justified" termination requires specific documented cause. "Unjustified" requires: 3 months constitutional indemnification + 20 days per year of seniority + proportional benefits. Reinstatement possible. |
| **Probation** | 30 days (up to 180 for management) -- relatively new concept (2012 reform) |
| **EOR complexity** | 4/5 -- CFDI electronic payroll receipts, SBC (base contribution salary) calculation, mandatory profit sharing (PTU, 10% of pre-tax profits), Christmas bonus, and bi-monthly pay cycle. |
| **Common pitfalls** | SBC (Salario Base de Cotizacion) calculation for IMSS; CFDI payroll receipt compliance (real-time XML); aguinaldo (Christmas bonus -- min 15 days salary by Dec 20); PTU (profit sharing); vacation premium (25% of vacation days pay); state payroll tax variation |
